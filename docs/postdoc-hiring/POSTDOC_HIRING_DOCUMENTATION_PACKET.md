# Documentation Packet for Current Postdoctoral / Research Associate Recruitment

Prepared for the current hiring cycle associated with Tianhao Wang's postdoctoral / research associate recruitment.

## 1. Purpose

This packet consolidates the core documentation needed to run the current search in a structured, fair, and well-documented way. It is designed to support the posting, screening, interviewing, and onboarding stages of the hire.

The packet covers:

- search scope and role goals
- search governance and fairness principles
- structured screening and interview documentation
- evaluation rubric and decision process
- mentoring and onboarding plan
- recordkeeping checklist

## 2. Search Scope and Role Goals

The position is intended for a postdoctoral-level research hire or research associate hire supporting Tianhao Wang's research program. The hire is expected to contribute to one or more active research projects, help move prototypes and experimental systems forward, collaborate with current lab members, and support paper- and project-facing execution.

Core goals for the hire:

- contribute research ideas and technical execution with limited day-to-day supervision
- implement, test, and refine research prototypes
- engage thoughtfully with privacy, security, and data systems style research questions
- collaborate effectively with the PI and other project participants
- communicate progress clearly in meetings, writing, and technical discussions

## 3. Search Governance and Fairness Principles

The search will be run using a structured, job-related, and consistently documented process.

Working principles:

- candidates are evaluated against role-relevant criteria rather than informal impressions
- the same core interview topics are used across candidates
- notes are recorded in a way that can explain the basis of decisions
- conflicts of interest or prior close advising relationships are flagged and managed
- final decisions rely on overall fit, research readiness, communication, independence, and collaboration potential

Operational safeguards:

- use a common interview question set for all finalists
- keep written notes for screening and interviews
- avoid off-the-cuff criteria that were not applied to other candidates
- separate "known personally" from "job-relevant evidence"
- document any recusal or limited-participation decisions if they arise

## 4. Screening Process

### 4.1 Initial file review

Each application is reviewed for:

- research area alignment with the position
- evidence of technical depth
- publication or project track record
- independence and execution ability
- communication clarity in materials
- fit with the lab's current project needs

### 4.2 Shortlisting

Candidates move to interview if they show a credible combination of:

- strong research fit
- evidence they can execute independently
- clear potential to contribute in the first few months
- no obvious mismatch with the position's technical direction

### 4.3 Review notes

For each screened candidate, reviewers should record:

- one-line overall recommendation: advance / hold / decline
- 2-4 concrete strengths
- 1-3 concrete concerns or open questions
- whether the concerns can be resolved in interview

## 5. Structured Interview Plan

All finalists should be asked substantially the same core questions, with follow-up only as needed to clarify research fit or logistics.

### Core interview questions

1. Please describe one recent project where you drove the technical work end to end. What was your contribution, what was difficult, and what would you change now?
2. Tell us about a research problem you are excited to work on next and why you think it is important.
3. Describe a time when an experiment, prototype, or system did not behave as expected. How did you debug it?
4. What kinds of collaboration structures help you do your best work?
5. How do you balance research exploration with the need to ship a result on a deadline?
6. If you joined this group, what would you want to accomplish in the first three months?

### Optional role-specific follow-ups

- project-specific technical questions tied to current lab work
- questions about systems-building, implementation, or evaluation design
- questions about writing, collaboration, or mentoring readiness
- visa / start-date / logistics questions if needed

### Interview documentation expectations

For each interview, the interviewer or committee should record:

- date and participants
- the questions actually asked
- a short note under each evaluation dimension
- an overall recommendation with confidence level

## 6. Evaluation Rubric

Candidates should be assessed on the following dimensions using a simple 1-5 scale.

### Dimension 1: Research fit

- 5: directly aligned with current and near-term lab needs
- 3: generally relevant, but needs clearer mapping to projects
- 1: weak alignment

### Dimension 2: Technical depth and execution

- 5: strong evidence of independent technical ownership
- 3: technically capable but ownership level is unclear
- 1: limited evidence of end-to-end execution

### Dimension 3: Research independence

- 5: consistently drives problems and next steps without heavy prompting
- 3: some independence but may need moderate structure
- 1: appears heavily dependent on others for direction

### Dimension 4: Communication

- 5: clear, concise, and thoughtful in explaining work
- 3: understandable but sometimes vague or disorganized
- 1: difficult to follow or unable to explain decisions clearly

### Dimension 5: Collaboration and professionalism

- 5: likely to work smoothly with the group and external collaborators
- 3: generally workable with some risk areas
- 1: likely to create friction or coordination problems

### Dimension 6: Near-term readiness

- 5: could contribute quickly with minimal ramp-up
- 3: would need some ramp-up but is still a plausible fit
- 1: likely too far from near-term needs

## 7. Decision Process

The final recommendation should be based on the totality of evidence rather than a single strong or weak signal.

Decision categories:

- strong yes
- yes
- mixed / hold
- no

Short written justification should include:

- why the candidate is or is not a fit now
- whether concerns are fundamental or trainable
- whether the candidate fits the current project timeline

## 8. Mentoring and Onboarding Plan

The existing mentoring plan can be reused and lightly tailored for this hire. The following plan is appropriate for the current search.

### First month

- set project scope and immediate deliverables
- review relevant papers, codebases, and datasets
- establish weekly meeting cadence with the PI
- introduce the hire to current collaborators and lab workflows
- define a concrete first technical milestone

### First three months

- complete at least one clearly scoped research or prototype milestone
- participate in regular project meetings
- produce progress updates in a repeatable format
- identify one paper- or project-facing deliverable
- clarify authorship, collaboration, and communication expectations

### Ongoing mentoring structure

- weekly 1:1 with the PI
- regular written or slide-based progress updates
- ad hoc technical unblock sessions as needed
- feedback on writing, experiments, and research direction
- support for conference submissions, talks, and professional development when appropriate
